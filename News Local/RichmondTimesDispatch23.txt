Poll hours

Tuesday, 6 a.m. to 7 p.m.

(Anyone in line at 7 p.m. is to be allowed to vote.)

Weather

The forecast for Richmond on Tuesday calls for partly cloudy skies with a morning low in the mid 40s rising to near 70 in the afternoon.

Poll information

Bring a photo ID. Voters in the Richmond area will cast paper ballots. They will fill in ovals and insert the ballots into optical scan machines.

What's on the ballot?

Five presidential candidates are on the ballot: Democrat Hillary Clinton, Republican Donald Trump, Libertarian Gary Johnson, independent Evan McMullin and Green Party nominee Jill Stein.

Voters also will choose members of the House of Representatives. Most voters in the Richmond area will have a new member of Congress because of redistricting.

Across Virginia, there also will be two constitutional amendments on the ballot.

In Richmond, voters will choose the city's new mayor among eight candidates on the ballot, though three of those candidates have withdrawn. Voters also will choose members of the City Council and the School Board in all nine districts.

In Petersburg, voters will determine City Council members in three districts and School Board members in three districts.

In Hopewell, voters will pick members of the City Council.

In Colonial Heights, voters will select City Council and School Board members.
